How to Ask for and Give Directions in German

Even with GPS in your pocket, knowing how to ask for and understand directions in German prevents you from becoming completely dependent on your phone—and it helps a lot when the signal fails or you need to quickly confirm something with someone on the street.

How to Ask

  • Entschuldigung, wie komme ich zum/zur...? — Excuse me, how do I get to...?
  • Wo ist die nächste Bushaltestelle? — Where is the nearest bus stop?
  • Ist es weit von hier? — Is it far from here?
  • Kann ich zu Fuß dorthin gehen? — Can I walk there?
Gehen Sie geradeaus und biegen Sie dann links ab.
Go straight ahead and then turn left.

Essential Directions Vocabulary

  • geradeaus — straight ahead
  • links / rechts — left / right
  • abbiegen — to turn (separable verb: biegen Sie ab)
  • an der Ampel — at the traffic light
  • an der Ecke — at the corner
  • gegenüber von... — across from...

Understanding quick responses

Germans, especially in big cities, tend to give directions very directly and quickly—feel free to ask them to repeat themselves without hesitation: "Können Sie das bitte wiederholen?" (Could you repeat that, please?) or Können Sie langsamer sprechen? (Could you speak more slowly?).

A practical tip: confirm by repeating

Repeating the directions you’ve received out loud (also, geradeaus und dann links, richtig? — so, straight ahead and then left, right?) helps you confirm that you understood correctly and practices active speech at the same time.
