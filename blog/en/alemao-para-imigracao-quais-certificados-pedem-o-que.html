German for immigration: which certificates are required and what

Each type of visa or immigration process for Germany requires a different level of proven German proficiency—and getting this wrong can cost you time and money if you obtain the wrong certificate. This is just a general overview; always confirm the current requirements with an immigration lawyer or the embassy.

The Most Widely Recognized Certificates

  • Goethe-Zertifikat — the most traditional and widely accepted, available from A1 to C2
  • TestDaF — specifically designed for those planning to study at a German university (equivalent to B2/C1)
  • telc Deutsch — a recognized alternative, with specific exams such as the "telc Deutsch B1 · Zertifikat Integration Deutsch," used in integration processes
  • DSH — an exam administered by the German university itself, generally for those already in the country

Overview by type of application (always check current requirements)

  • Family reunification visa (spouse): typically requires proof of a basic language level even before departure, usually A1.
  • Skilled worker visas: vary widely—some do not require any German if the employment contract is already in English, while others require B1/B2.
  • Citizenship (Einbürgerung): generally requires proof of B1, in addition to other requirements such as length of residence.
  • Undergraduate studies: typically require C1, demonstrated via TestDaF or DSH.

Why starting early matters

Reaching a solid B1/B2 level takes months of consistent study—immigration processes often have deadlines, and leaving language study to the last minute is one of the most common (and avoidable) mistakes made by those planning to move.
