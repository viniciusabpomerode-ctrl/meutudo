How to Apologize and Say Thank You the Right Way in German

Danke and Entschuldigung cover the basics, but German has variations in intensity and formality for expressing thanks and apologizing that make a real difference in a natural conversation.

Levels of “Thank You”

  • Danke — thank you, the neutral standard for any situation
  • Danke schön — thank you (more polite/emphatic), very common in stores and when receiving services
  • Vielen Dank — thank you very much, more formal and sincere
  • Danke vielmals — thank you very, very much, used when someone has really helped a lot
Vielen Dank für Ihre Hilfe!
Thank you very much for your help!

How to respond to a thank you

  • Bitte / Bitte schön — you’re welcome
  • Gern geschehen — my pleasure / it was my pleasure to help
  • Kein Problem — no problem

Levels of "apology"

  • Entschuldigung — the standard term, used to get someone’s attention or offer a light apology (“excuse me,” “sorry”)
  • Es tut mir leid — I’m sorry, used for a more sincere and heartfelt apology
  • Verzeihung — pardon, more formal/polite than Entschuldigung
  • Entschuldigen Sie bitte — formal version (with “Sie”), used in professional contexts

The difference that changes the tone of the phrase

Entschuldigung is almost automatic (even used to ask for space on the bus), while es tut mir leid carries real emotional weight—using the wrong one in the wrong context can sound exaggerated or, conversely, too superficial.
